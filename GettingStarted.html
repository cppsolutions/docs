Before you begin using CPP Analyzer

You should first obtain a copy of the clients statement of contributions (SOC) from them. They can obtain their SOC from Service Canada.


Once you have obtained a SOC from the client there are two ways to enter pensionable earnings into CPP Analyzer:


1.The Client can print or order a paper copy form Service Canada and then forward it to you, in this case you will have to manually enter the information.

2.You can instruct the client on  how to save a HTML (copy) of their (SOC).


The following client information is needed for client data input:

For each scenario:

•The age the client plans to begin their CPP benefit,

•The amount of income your client will make from now until they plan to begin their CPP benefit,

•Post CPP benefit income amount: If they are planning to work after they begin their CPP benefit and contribute to their Post Retirement Benefit (PRB).


For both scenarios (common for both):

•Statement of Contributions ( Service Canada).

•Estimate of the client's life expectancy.

•For the Survivor Benefit calculation: Estimate of their spouses life expectancy.

•If your client is eligible for the Child Rearing Dropout provision - the birthdates of all their children.

•Estimate of Inflation increases of future CPP benefits (CPI).

•Estimate of Increases of future average wages (Annual Yearly Maximum Pensionable earnings (YMPE)).
